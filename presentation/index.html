STEP 01 / CAPTURE — ZERO TYPING

She photographs it, or just speaks it.

A page of her bahi-khata, a customer's bill, or a wholesaler challan — Claude Opus 4.8 vision reads the Hindi/Hinglish handwriting; a fine-tuned whisper.cpp Hindi model handles voice on-device. Capture is mode-aware: a daily scribble becomes ledger entries, a customer bill posts to that customer's udhaar, a challan becomes priced stock. No forms, no jargon.

STEP 02 / UNDERSTAND

Paper becomes a live ledger.

Entries auto-build a daily P&L (sales, COGS, net, cash vs udhaar), an inventory catalogue (stock, cost→sell margin, low-stock alerts) and an udhaar tracker. Every sale line snapshots its price & cost, so margins stay accurate even when catalogue prices drift — credit-grade data, not estimates.

STEP 03 / ADVISE

It stops being a ledger.

Four AI agents — Demand, Margin, Competition, Strategy — brief her daily. On top sits an agentic assistant: she asks anything in her own language and a tool-calling model queries her live data and answers in Hinglish with inline charts — what's selling, who owes most, what to reorder. It can also turn a spoken sentence into a sale or repayment she confirms.

STEP 04 / SELL

One tap — "Go online."

Her live inventory publishes as a neighbourhood storefront (delivery / pick-on-arrival). Orders land on an owner page she opens inside the app. Built from data she already has, it's her counter-punch to quick commerce.

STEP 05 / CREDIT

The asset she never had.

Every recorded rupee feeds an Artha Score (0–100) + an indicative working-capital band — an alternative-data credit profile from real commerce flow (sales consistency, margin, cost control, udhaar recovery). The asset a bank could never see on paper. NBFC-partnered origination is the roadmap and the business model.
